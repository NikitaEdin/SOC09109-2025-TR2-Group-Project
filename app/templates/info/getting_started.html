Get Started

Different Types of Flights

Urban

Projects situated in highly populated, built-up areas where police must be informed before flying, and a leaflet drop is often required.

Rural

Projects located in open or remote areas where wildlife restrictions apply, and a loading list is typically necessary.

Project Creation Process

1) Initiate Project: Log in, navigate to the dashboard, and click the green "New Project" button.
2) Operation Location: Select the location on the map by clicking on the desired point.
3) Project Type: Select either "Rural" or "Urban," depending on the type of project.
4) Project Details: Enter the details of the project including the title, description, date, and project purpose.
5) Project Features: Select any features relevant to the project by toggling the options.
6) Finalise Project: View the project in the dashboard, where you can also edit details or manage user access.

Project Purposes

1) Practice: Use drones to improve piloting skills and build confidence with the equipment.
2) Research: Use drones to test new ideas or for gathering useful information.
3) Training: Learn how to operate drones and develop skills for different purposes.
4) Other: Adapt drone use for unique needs, allowing room for creativity.

Timeline Information

28 Days in Advance: Preparation begins by ensuring all necessary permissions are obtained.
7 Days in Advance: A week before the flight, you'll focus on the practical details: conducting evaluations, customising the loading list, completing the viability study, finalising risk analysis, and notifying any relevant authorities or communities about your operation. It's also a good time to monitor the weather and make early adjustments to your plans if needed.
24 Hours in Advance: Contact ATC if operating in restricted zones and decide go/no-go based on weather. Review the loading list, send crew call sheets, update firmware, charge equipment, and finalise preparations. Customise and print key documentation, including emergency procedures and checklists. Conduct a test flight if necessary, ensuring all equipment and crew coordination are ready for the operation.
Day of Flight: On the day of the flight, log into the operator account and pack equipment using the loading list. Check NOTAMs, complete the site checklist, and decide go/no-go based on conditions. Assemble the UAS, brief the crew, and complete pre-flight checks. After the mission, perform post-flight actions, disassemble the UAS, and pack all equipment properly.
